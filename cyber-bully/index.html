The game

The world is already ending.

Cyber Bully places the player in Low Haven, a collapsing mega city viewed through a late 1980s vision of the future. You control one of the last functioning soldiers, kept alive by emergency bodily augmentations, moving through a procedurally generated city filled with automated enemies, decaying military systems, and obsolete technology that continues to operate without purpose.

No Recovery

Lore

The final record.

29 August 1994 / 04:11 UTC

World War Three begins. What starts as a conventional global conflict collapses into something far larger. Front lines form across continents, cities harden overnight, and airspace becomes permanent fire. The arms race accelerates beyond control, with weapons conceived, built, and deployed in the same breath.

Three powers dominate the final escalation. The Iron Pact controls the Atlantic world through orbital dominance, autonomous armour, and continent scale missile grids. The Solar Dominion rises across the Pacific through rapid AI iteration, mass drone warfare, and experimental gravity based weapons. The Black Meridian consolidates vast resource regions, building deep strike deterrents, hardened infrastructure, and last resort planetary systems.

Diplomacy fails faster than it can be attempted. Every escalation removes the option of restraint.

2 September 1994 / 19:38 UTC

Orbital combat spreads beyond satellites. Lunar facilities are struck during a Solar Dominion offensive. The Moon fractures in full view of Earth, and global gravitational instability begins immediately.

4 September 1994 / 05:26 UTC

The Iron Pact authorises planetary scale retaliation. A high energy weapon fires during a failed Meridian command decapitation strike. A section of the Earth is removed.

Atmospheric collapse begins within hours. Air pressure fails unevenly. Firestorms tear through front lines and cities alike. Oceans recede. Communications fragment and fall silent.

Military bases become the last centres of command. Some are destroyed outright. Others collapse internally as automated defence systems, reactors, and countermeasures operate without oversight.

Imperial cities receive no special consideration. Low Haven is one of them: an administrative city, a logistics hub, a place designed to persist, not to endure. Its defences are procedural, its authority symbolic, and its infrastructure is already failing before the shockwave reaches it.

There is no evacuation order. There is no counterstrike. There is no final stand. Low Haven does not fall. It is simply no longer relevant.

7 September 1994 / 01:14 UTC

All coordinated warfare ceases. Not through surrender. Not through victory. Because the planet can no longer sustain conflict.

Emergency bodily augmentations are deployed globally to extend combat operability for days in environments no longer fit for human life. Oxygen, circulation, neural activity, pain: enough to function, not enough to survive.

Low Haven receives the same treatment as everywhere else. Temporary systems. Failing systems. Systems designed to extract a few more hours of utility from bodies that are already finished.

This record exists only because the systems that created it have not yet shut down. What follows takes place in the Earth's final hours. There will be no recovery. There will be no witnesses. Nothing matters.

Setting

Late Cold War systems at the end of the world.

Cyber Bully takes place in the final, dying days of the world, viewed through a retro futuristic late 1980s vision of the future shaped by Cold War paranoia, analogue machinery, and crude digital systems. World War Three erased civilisation almost as soon as it began, shattering the Moon in the opening strikes and tearing a section of the planet away shortly after, leaving the sky fractured and wrong. The atmosphere began collapsing within hours as firestorms, pressure failures, and sudden freezes swept across the surface. Military bases, the last centres of command, were destroyed in the same narrow window, either hit directly or lost to internal collapse as automated defence systems and reactors failed without oversight. The soldiers still moving through the city are alive only through emergency bodily augmentations designed to keep them operational for days, regulating oxygen, circulation, neural activity, and pain suppression while identity and memory degrade. Everything is mediated through flickering CRT interfaces, decaying military operating systems, and industrial hardware built with late Cold War logic, with no aftermath, no recovery, and no future beyond obsolete systems executing their final routines as the planet dies in real time.
