The studio

Games, smart toys and interactive technology built through research.

Kestrel Kinetics Research & Technology is an independent development and research studio creating original games, smart toys, interactive products and experimental technologies. Our work combines creative design, physical computing, robotics, software development and academic research to build products that entertain, respond, adapt and encourage meaningful interaction — developed as complete experiences that also serve as practical platforms for research and academic publication.

What we build

Research-informed, across every medium.

01

Our Approach

Kestrel Kinetics combines creative development with structured research, experimentation and iterative testing. Features and technologies are designed, prototyped, implemented and evaluated through a research-informed process that investigates how people interact with digital characters, physical devices, simulated environments and responsive products — work that can contribute to academic papers, technical reports and conference presentations.

02

Original Games

We develop original games with distinctive worlds, characters and interconnected gameplay systems, exploring procedural environments, reactive characters, simulated routines, extensive customisation, environmental interaction and emergent gameplay. Rather than treating these elements as isolated features, we design them to work together and create experiences that respond to player decisions.

03

Smart Toys and Interactive Products

Kestrel Kinetics develops smart toys and interactive products that combine physical objects with sensors, embedded electronics, software and responsive behaviour, reacting to movement, sound, touch, proximity, environmental conditions or user input to support play, learning, companionship, creativity, accessibility or animal care. Our aim is to create physical products that feel engaging and responsive while remaining practical, understandable and enjoyable to use.

04

Robotics and Physical Computing

Our work includes robotics, embedded systems and connected devices — prototypes that use motors, sensors, cameras, microcontrollers, single-board computers and custom software to interact with people, animals and their surroundings. Projects may range from experimental robotic toys to interactive care systems, educational devices and autonomous prototypes.

05

Cyber Bully: 502 Bad Gateway

Cyber Bully: 502 Bad Gateway is a retro-futuristic action game set in Low Haven, a collapsing mega city during the final hours of Earth, featuring a semi-procedural city, thousands of possible armour and item combinations, interactive terminal systems and reactive NPCs with individual identities and stories. Its major systems are developed through a research-informed process that may also become subjects for formal investigation and academic publication.

06

Manic Monday

Manic Monday is a school life simulation built around character routines, needs, social behaviour and card-based interaction, operating as an interconnected simulation in which students and staff follow schedules, respond to changing circumstances and interact with the environment around them. The project explores how character systems, player decisions and everyday routines can produce believable and unexpected situations within a structured virtual environment.

07

Research and Publication

Research is integrated directly into the studio's development process. Features created for our games, smart toys and interactive technologies may be examined through formal studies covering their design, implementation, evaluation and effect on user experience, contributing to academic papers, conference presentations and wider discussions surrounding game design, robotics, interaction design, smart products and emerging technology.

08

Technology Development

Alongside finished products, Kestrel Kinetics develops reusable tools, systems and technical prototypes — procedural generation tools, character behaviour systems, terminal interfaces, modular customisation frameworks, robotic control systems, sensor-based interactions and connected hardware. Technology developed for one project may be refined and adapted for future games, smart toys, research projects or commercial collaborations.

09

Independent Development

Operating independently allows Kestrel Kinetics to explore ideas that may not fit established commercial formulas, supporting experimentation with unusual mechanics, responsive physical products, unconventional themes and technically ambitious systems. Our objective is to create distinctive products that function as entertainment, practical technology and meaningful research.

10

Collaboration

Kestrel Kinetics is open to collaboration with universities, researchers, developers, engineers, artists, manufacturers and technology partners, on game development, smart toy design, robotics, research projects, prototype development, student engagement, product testing and academic publication.

11

Our Aim

Kestrel Kinetics aims to create original games, smart toys and interactive technologies that combine entertainment, technical experimentation and academic research — each project designed to provide a distinctive experience while contributing to a wider understanding of games, robotics, responsive products and human interaction with technology.
